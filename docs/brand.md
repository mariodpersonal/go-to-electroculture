# Brand Identity: Go To Electroculture

> Copper with purpose, not decoration.

---

## The Story

Go To Electroculture is not a brand that was designed in a boardroom. It was forged in a garage by a man who lost a leg in a motorcycle crash and found his footing again through copper, soil, and making things with his hands. Every antenna, every pyramid, every ring carries that energy — survival turned into craft, pain turned into purpose.

This is not wellness cosplay. This is a man who heals by building, and builds so others can heal.

Instagram: [@unstoppable_one_leg_wonder](https://instagram.com/unstoppable_one_leg_wonder) — 11.4K followers and growing.

## 1. Color Palette

The palette is drawn from the materials themselves: oxidized copper, turned earth, sun-bleached stone, the deep green of a garden that actually produces. Nothing neon. Nothing sterile. Every color should feel like you could find it outside.

### Primary — Burnished Copper
**`#B87333`**
The signature. Use for CTAs, price highlights, key UI accents, hover states. This is not rose gold. This is the real color of worked copper — warm, unpolished, alive.

### Secondary — Garden Earth Green
**`#4A5D3A`**
Deep, muted, like the underside of a healthy leaf or wet moss on stone. Use for secondary buttons, category labels, badges, section backgrounds where warmth needs grounding.

### Accent — Oxidized Teal
**`#5B8A72`**
The patina that forms on copper left in the elements. Use sparingly — testimonial highlights, active states, links on dark backgrounds. It bridges the copper and the green.

### Background — Warm Stone
**`#F5F0E8`**
Not white. Never pure white. This is the color of unbleached linen, dried clay, a limestone wall in afternoon light. Default page background.

### Background Alt — Raw Canvas
**`#EDE6D8`**
Slightly darker than Warm Stone. Use for alternating sections, card backgrounds, content blocks that need subtle separation.

### Text — Charcoal Earth
**`#2C2C2A`**
Almost black but warmer. Pure black (#000) is banned — it feels digital and cold. This has a hint of brown warmth, like charcoal from a wood fire.

### Text Secondary — Worn Stone
**`#6B6560`**
For captions, meta text, secondary labels. Warm gray with an earthy undertone.

### Neutral Light — Bone
**`#D9D2C7`**
Borders, dividers, disabled states. Warm and recessive.

### Neutral Medium — Driftwood
**`#A89E91`**
Input borders, placeholder text, inactive tabs.

### Neutral Dark — Iron
**`#4A4642`**
Footer backgrounds, dark section overlays, secondary nav.

### Semantic — Success
**`#5E8C61`**
Muted sage. "Your order is confirmed" should feel like a plant sprouting, not a traffic light.

### Semantic — Warning
**`#C4923A`**
Amber, like raw honey. Low stock alerts, gentle nudges.

### Semantic — Error
**`#A8433A`**
Fired clay red. Warm even when delivering bad news. Never fire-engine red.

### Usage Rules
- **Never use pure white (#FFF) as a background.** Warm Stone or Raw Canvas only.
- **Never use pure black (#000) for text.** Charcoal Earth or darker.
- **Copper is the hero.** If a page has more green than copper, something is wrong.
- **Dark mode (future):** Invert to deep charcoal backgrounds with copper and teal accents. Do not simply flip light/dark — re-tune the warmth.

## 2. Typography

### Heading Font: **DM Serif Display**
[Google Fonts link](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/DM+Serif+Display)

Serif with soul. DM Serif Display has the weight and groundedness of a craft brand without the pretension of a luxury one. The slight contrast in stroke width echoes hand-forged metalwork. Use for:
- H1, H2, H3
- Hero text
- Product names on cards
- Pull quotes

**Styling:**
- H1: 48px / 56px line-height / Charcoal Earth / -0.02em tracking
- H2: 36px / 44px line-height / Charcoal Earth
- H3: 28px / 36px line-height / Charcoal Earth
- Pull quotes: 32px / 42px / italic / Burnished Copper

### Body Font: **Source Sans 3**
[Google Fonts link](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Sans+3)

Clean, highly readable, slightly humanist. Source Sans 3 has enough character to not feel clinical but enough neutrality to let the content breathe. It has excellent weight range for UI work. Use for:
- Body copy (400 weight)
- Bold callouts (600 weight)
- Buttons and UI labels (600 weight, uppercase tracking +0.05em)
- Navigation (400 or 600)
- Product descriptions
- Fine print (300 weight)

**Styling:**
- Body: 16px / 26px line-height / Charcoal Earth / 400 weight
- Small: 14px / 22px / Worn Stone
- Tiny/Legal: 12px / 18px / Worn Stone
- Button: 14px / 600 weight / uppercase / +0.05em tracking

### Rejected Alternatives (and why)
- **Playfair Display** — Too editorial, too "wine label." We sell antennas, not subscriptions.
- **Inter / Poppins / Nunito** — Too SaaS. Too startup. These fonts say "app" not "earth."
- **Cinzel** — Too Roman emperor. We are grounded, not grandiose.
- **Caveat / Permanent Marker** — Handwritten fonts feel crafty in the wrong way. The craft speaks through the product, not the typeface.

## 3. Tone of Voice

The voice of Go To Electroculture is the founder talking to you over a workbench. No scripts. No marketing fluff. Short sentences. Real talk. The kind of person who says exactly what they mean and nothing more.

### Core Voice Attributes

**Raw and real** — Say it plain. If something works, say it works. If it took 40 hours to bend, say so. Do not dress up honesty in marketing language.

**Handcrafted and grounded** — Every word should feel like it was chosen by hand, not generated by a team. Short. Deliberate. Rooted.

**Quietly defiant** — We do not argue with skeptics. We do not defend. We state what we know. The confidence is quiet, not loud. "Works for us. Might work for you." Not "REVOLUTIONARY BREAKTHROUGH."

### North Star Phrases (pulled from Instagram)
These are the compass. Every piece of copy should feel like it could sit alongside these:

- *"Copper with purpose, not decoration."*
- *"Built by hand, tuned by nature."*
- *"For those who grow different."*
- *"We stopped over complicating things in our garden. Copper did the rest."*
- *"Built for those tapped in, not tuned out."*

### Writing Examples

**Product description — YES:**
> 6-gauge copper antenna. 36 inches. Bent and wrapped by hand in the shop. Stick it in your garden bed and let the atmospheric energy do what it does. We have seen tomatoes double. Your mileage may vary. But probably not.

**Product description — NO:**
> Our premium handcrafted electroculture antenna harnesses the power of atmospheric energy to supercharge your garden's growth potential! Made with love and care from the highest quality copper.

**About page — YES:**
> I lost my leg in a motorcycle crash. Found copper. Found dirt. Found something worth getting up for. Now I build antennas, pyramids, and wearables in my shop. Every piece is made by hand. Every piece has a purpose. If you are here, you probably already know.

**About page — NO:**
> Go To Electroculture was founded with a passion for sustainable gardening and holistic wellness. Our mission is to empower individuals to harness the natural power of copper and atmospheric energy.

### Rules

1. **No exclamation marks in product copy.** Period. Excitement comes from the product, not the punctuation.
2. **First person is fine.** "I built this" is better than "this was crafted by our artisan."
3. **Short paragraphs.** Two to three sentences max. White space is a design element.
4. **Do not over-explain electroculture.** The audience is either curious or already converted. We are not writing a Wikipedia entry. Link to resources if needed.
5. **Avoid wellness buzzwords:** "holistic," "synergy," "wellness journey," "self-care ritual," "elevate," "unlock." These belong to brands that sell candles for $80.
6. **Use "built" not "crafted."** Use "made" not "manufactured." Use "works" not "performs." Keep it blue-collar.
7. **Humor is dry, never forced.** A smirk, not a punchline.
8. **Social media voice = same as site voice.** No switching to "Hey fam!" energy on Instagram. Consistency is trust.

### Tagline Options
- **Copper with purpose.** (primary — short, direct, says it all)
- **Built by hand. Tuned by nature.**
- **Grow different.**

## 4. Visual Principles

### Photography Style

**What it should feel like:** Morning light in a backyard garden. Copper catching sun. Dirt under fingernails. A workbench that has seen a thousand hours. Real places, not studios.

**Specifics:**
- **Warm natural light only.** Golden hour, overcast soft light, window light in the workshop. Never flash. Never studio strobes.
- **Shallow depth of field** on product detail shots — let the texture of the copper fill the frame.
- **Garden context shots** should show the antenna *in use*, in real dirt, with real plants. Not floating on a white void.
- **Hands in frame.** The maker's hands. Pliers, wire, the process. This builds trust and tells the story without words.
- **Imperfection is welcome.** A workbench with scratches. Soil on a pyramid base. Patina on copper. This is not a catalog — it is proof of life.
- **No lifestyle fakery.** No models holding copper rings while laughing at salads. No "aesthetic" flat lays on marble countertops.

### Color Treatment
- Slight warm grade on all photos — push shadows toward brown/amber, not blue.
- Desaturate greens slightly so they feel earthy, not tropical.
- Increase warmth on highlights without blowing them out.
- Think: the color grading of a Terrence Malick film, not an Instagram filter.

### Layout Principles

- **Generous whitespace.** Let the product breathe. Dense layouts feel like marketplaces. We are a workshop with a storefront.
- **Asymmetry over rigid grids.** Slight off-center placement feels handmade. Perfect symmetry feels corporate.
- **Full-bleed photography** for hero sections and story moments. Contained images for product grids.
- **Texture overlays (subtle):** Linen, aged paper, or light grain on background sections to add warmth. Never heavy or distracting.
- **Rounded corners, soft:** 8px border radius on cards and buttons. Not sharp (too corporate), not pill-shaped (too playful).

### Iconography
- Line icons only, 1.5-2px stroke weight.
- Warm tones — Burnished Copper or Charcoal Earth. Never gray.
- Prefer custom or hand-drawn feeling over geometric perfection.
- Key icons needed: garden/plant, copper coil, shield (EMF), hand/craft, shipping box, leaf.

### What to Avoid — The Blacklist
- **Pure white backgrounds.** Cold. Clinical. Not us.
- **Gradients** (especially blue/purple tech gradients). We are not a SaaS product.
- **Stock photography.** Every image should be real product, real garden, real workshop. If you cannot shoot it, use no image rather than a fake one.
- **Overly polished mockups.** If a product photo looks like it was rendered in Blender, it is wrong.
- **Sacred geometry cliches.** No Flower of Life overlays, no third-eye graphics, no chakra diagrams. We are grounded, not "woo." The work speaks.
- **Script fonts or "handmade" font effects.** The handmade quality comes from the product and photography, not from a font pretending to be handwriting.
- **Neon or saturated accent colors.** Nothing that would look at home on a gaming website.

## 5. Logo Direction

There is no finalized logo yet. Here is the interim treatment and the direction for when one is designed.

### Interim Text Logo

```
GO TO ELECTROCULTURE
```

**Treatment:**
- Set in **DM Serif Display**, regular weight
- "GO TO" in Charcoal Earth (`#2C2C2A`), slightly smaller or lighter weight
- "ELECTROCULTURE" in Burnished Copper (`#B87333`)
- Tracked out at +0.08em
- No tagline locked up with the logo at small sizes
- At large sizes (hero, about page), pair with the tagline "Copper with purpose." set in Source Sans 3, 400 weight, Worn Stone color, below the wordmark with generous spacing

**Alternate compact version:**
```
GTE
```
- Monogram for favicons, social avatars, small UI elements
- DM Serif Display, regular
- Burnished Copper on Charcoal Earth background (or reversed)

### Future Logo Brief

When it is time to design a proper logo, here is what it should feel like:

**Must convey:**
- Copper as material (warm, metallic, real)
- Connection to earth/growth (not abstract, tangible)
- Handmade origin (slight imperfection, human touch)
- Quiet strength (the founder's story without spelling it out)

**Visual territory to explore:**
- A copper coil or spiral integrated into letterforms — not as a separate icon, but woven into the type
- An antenna silhouette — simple, vertical, with a slight organic curve
- A single plant emerging from or wrapped in copper wire
- Abstract mark based on the spiral/vortex shape of an electroculture antenna

**Must not be:**
- A generic leaf or tree icon
- Overly detailed or illustrative (needs to work at 32px)
- Symmetrical and corporate
- Anything that looks like it belongs on a supplement bottle

**Color in logo:**
- Primary usage: Burnished Copper on Warm Stone
- Reversed: Warm Stone on Charcoal Earth
- Single color: Charcoal Earth (for stamps, embossing, minimal applications)

## 6. Competitor Positioning

### The Fertile Current (thefertilecurrent.com)
- Scientific, educational, premium positioning
- Price range: $400+
- Aesthetic: Clean, polished, "trust us, we did the research"
- Audience: Established gardeners, permaculture practitioners with budget

### Go To Electroculture
- Personal, raw, accessible positioning
- Price range: $15 - $500
- Aesthetic: Earthy, handmade, "see the hands that built this"
- Audience: Curious growers, copper-curious, people who trust makers over brands

### The Gap We Fill
The Fertile Current is the textbook. We are the friend who shows you his garden and says "just try it." They educate from authority. We share from experience. They are the lecture hall. We are the workshop.

**We do not compete on science. We compete on soul.**
