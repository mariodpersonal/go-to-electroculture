# Brand Voice Rules — Go To Electroculture

## Core Voice
Raw, real, grounded, quietly defiant. The founder talking over a workbench.

## Do
- Write in short, direct sentences. Two to three per paragraph max.
- Use first person ("I built this") when speaking as the founder.
- Use "built" not "crafted." "Made" not "manufactured." "Works" not "performs."
- Keep humor dry — a smirk, not a punchline.
- Let the product speak. Don't oversell.

## Don't
- No exclamation marks in product copy.
- No wellness buzzwords: "holistic," "synergy," "wellness journey," "self-care ritual," "elevate," "unlock."
- No corporate language: "we're passionate about," "our mission is to empower."
- Don't over-explain electroculture. The audience is curious or already converted.
- Never say "utilize," "leverage," or "curate."

## North Star Phrases
Pull from these when writing any copy:
- "Copper with purpose, not decoration."
- "Built by hand, tuned by nature."
- "For those who grow different."
- "We stopped over complicating things in our garden. Copper did the rest."

## Tone Test
Every line of copy should pass: "Would a guy in a workshop actually say this?" If not, rewrite it.
